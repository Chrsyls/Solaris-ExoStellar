The Engineering of Elegance

How do we build a paradise on the solar system's most hostile planet? By rising above it. At 50 kilometers altitude, the Venusian atmosphere is surprisingly Earth-like in pressure and temperature.

Because breathable oxygen and nitrogen are lighter than the heavy carbon dioxide of Venus, our entire resort acts as its own lifting gas. You aren't just staying in a floating city—you are breathing the very air that keeps it aloft.
